Maintenance

EV maintenance is usually simpler than gas/ICE maintenance, but EVs are not maintenance-free. Tires, brakes, fluids, software, and service access still matter.

What usually changes with an EV

  • No oil changes: Battery-electric vehicles do not have engine oil, oil filters, spark plugs, exhaust systems, or many of the engine parts found in gas cars.
  • Fewer routine engine services: There is no gasoline engine tune-up schedule for a BEV. Plug-in hybrids still have a gas engine, so they keep some ICE maintenance.
  • Brake pads may last longer: Regenerative braking can reduce brake wear, but the brake system still needs inspection and occasional service.
  • Software matters more: Some updates, recalls, and charging behavior changes may come through software or dealer service.

What still needs attention

  • Tires: EVs can be heavy and have strong instant torque. Rotate tires, watch alignment, and expect tire choice to affect range, noise, and ride comfort.
  • Cabin air filter: Usually a simple scheduled replacement, similar to many gas cars.
  • Brake fluid and coolant: Follow the owner manual. Some EVs have coolant for the battery or power electronics.
  • 12-volt battery: Many EVs still use a small 12-volt battery for vehicle electronics. If it fails, the car may act dead even when the main battery is charged.
  • Wipers, washer fluid, suspension, and lights: Basic car maintenance still applies.

Gas/ICE maintenance to compare

  • Common ICE items: Oil changes, engine air filter, spark plugs, belts, exhaust, emissions equipment, transmission service, fuel system service, and engine cooling system service.
  • Plug-in hybrids: PHEVs may reduce gasoline use, but they still include both EV parts and gas-engine maintenance.
  • Best source: Use the vehicle owner manual or maintenance schedule for the exact model, trim, and year.
