EV Models

EV model availability changes often, so WESTERN EV points to current comparison sources and explains what shoppers should compare before getting attached to one model.

Current model sources

  • FuelEconomy.gov EV comparison: Official U.S. DOE and EPA source for comparing new and used all-electric cars, trucks, and SUVs by class, make, model year, range, drive type, and MPGe. Open FuelEconomy.gov EV comparison
  • Reviews page: Free outside sources for expert reviews, road tests, owner feedback, and model research. Open WESTERN EV Reviews
  • Automaker pages: Best for current trims, ordering status, connector details, warranty terms, and available charging offers.
  • Dealer inventory pages: Useful for seeing what is actually available locally, but verify trim, battery, range, and incentives carefully.

Comparison checklist

  • Body style: Small car, sedan, SUV/crossover, pickup, van, or luxury model.
  • Range: Compare official range with your daily driving, winter driving, elevation, and road-trip needs.
  • Charging: Look at home charging options, DC fast-charging speed, connector type, and network access.
  • Battery and warranty: Review battery warranty length, degradation coverage, and service access near you.
  • Cost: Compare purchase price, insurance, electricity, tires, registration, maintenance, and resale expectations.
  • Comfort and controls: Test seat comfort, visibility, physical controls, screen dependence, and phone/navigation integration.
