Route Segments

A simpler route-first view for western EV travel. These are draft corridor notes, not verified station recommendations yet.

Route Options

  • Salt Lake City to Sacramento (I-80)Draft

    Main idea: I-80 corridor across western Utah, northern Nevada, Reno/Sparks, and into northern California.

    Segment Destination Charger Optional Stops
    Salt Lake City to Wendovernotes Charger address to be added Salt Flats east rest stop slow charger, reported free; verify current status
    Wendover to Elkonotes Charger address to be added Wells partial top-off if wind, weather, or range margin is tight
    Elko to Winnemuccanotes Charger address to be added Battle Mountain partial top-off if needed
    Winnemucca to Reno / Sparksnotes Charger address to be added Lovelock partial top-off if needed
    Reno / Sparks to Sacramento / Rosevillenotes Charger address to be added Use Reno/Sparks or Sacramento-area backup planning before the climb
    Extra segment notes

    Field note: Salt Lake City to Wendover can become the risky leg in snow, strong headwinds, or crosswinds. A full charge from Salt Lake City is the baseline, and a few extra miles from the Salt Flats rest stop can be useful when conditions are rough.

    Full-charge baseline segments: Salt Lake City to Wendover, Wendover to Elko, Elko to Winnemucca, Winnemucca to Reno/Sparks, and Reno/Sparks toward Sacramento/Roseville.

    Partial top-off candidates: Salt Flats east rest stop, Wells, Battle Mountain, and Lovelock. These can fill gaps when speed, wind, or weather uses more range than expected. Verify current access, connector, app, and status before relying on any stop.

    Direction matters over the Sierra. Reno/Sparks to Sacramento/Roseville is often easier after reaching the summit because much of the route trends downhill. Sacramento/Roseville to Reno/Sparks can use far more energy because the climb comes before the summit and winter road checks may slow travel.

  • Salt Lake City to Los Angeles (I-15)Draft

    Main idea: I-15 corridor through southern Utah, Las Vegas, Mojave Desert, and into southern California.

    Segment Destination Charger Optional Stops
    Nephi areanotes Charger address to be added Spanish Fork or next I-15 DC fast option to verify
    Beaver areanotes Charger address to be added Cedar City or next I-15 DC fast option to verify
    Cedar City areanotes Charger address to be added Blink station at Ford, reported accessible 24/7
    St. George / Mesquitenotes Charger address to be added Backup before Las Vegas or desert leg
    Las Vegas areanotes Charger address to be added Alternate network or Tesla option
    Barstow / Victorvillenotes Charger address to be added Backup before Los Angeles traffic
    Extra segment notes

    Nephi can be useful as an early top-off before continuing south, especially for drivers who prefer to keep extra margin. Beaver and Cedar City should be treated as separate planning stops. Holiday traffic, desert heat, and station crowding can also matter on I-15. A backup stop is useful even when the planned station looks fine on a map.

    If a charger is listed at a dealership, do not assume it is usable just because it appears on a map. Some dealer chargers may be blocked after hours or may require an employee to activate. The Cedar City Ford backup is worth noting because it has been accessible even when the dealership was closed.

Route table rules

  • Goal: Fewer map pins, clearer route decisions.
  • Include: Primary stop, backup stop, access notes, connector notes, and route risk notes.
  • Dealer stops: Confirm public access, after-hours access, and whether staff activation is needed.
  • Exclude: Every charger in town. The traveler needs the dependable route choices first.
