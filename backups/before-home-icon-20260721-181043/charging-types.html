Charging Types

Charging type affects how quickly energy is added, where charging is practical, and what equipment or connector may be needed.

Basic levels

  • Level 1: Uses a standard household outlet. It is slow, but can work for light daily driving.
  • Level 2: Common for home, workplace, hotels, public parking, and longer stops. It is much faster than Level 1. Usually used for overnight charging.
  • DC fast charging: Designed for road trips and quick public stops. Usually found near highways, travel centers, grocery stores, shopping areas, and other places where drivers can stop briefly. Speed varies by vehicle, charger, temperature, and battery state.
  • Slow or fast? Location is a clue. Workplaces, hospitals, hotels, parking garages, and many store parking lots often have Level 2 chargers, which may take hours. Highway and travel-center chargers are more likely to be DC fast chargers. When in doubt, look for the posted kW: Level 2 is often single-digit or low double-digit kW, while DC fast chargers are usually much higher, such as 50 kW, 150 kW, or more.
  • Connector types: Vehicles and chargers may use different plug styles. Always check connector compatibility before relying on a station.
