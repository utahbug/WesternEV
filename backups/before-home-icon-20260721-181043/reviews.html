Reviews

Use established review sites to compare impressions, test results, comfort, controls, cargo space, range notes, and owner feedback. WESTERN EV should link to these sources rather than repeat their reviews.

Free review and research sources

  • Edmunds: EV rankings, full reviews, road-test notes, pricing context, and buying guides. Open Edmunds EV reviews
  • Car and Driver: Expert reviews, instrumented testing, first drives, comparisons, and EV news. Open Car and Driver EV coverage
  • Kelley Blue Book: Reviews, pricing research, 5-year cost-to-own information, and buyer guidance. Open Kelley Blue Book EV page
  • Cars.com: Expert reviews, shopping research, owner reviews, and inventory context. Open Cars.com EV research
  • MotorWeek: Long-running PBS road tests and video reviews. Open MotorWeek reviews
  • FuelEconomy.gov: Official U.S. DOE and EPA comparison data for range, MPGe, class, make, model year, and efficiency. This is not a review site, but it is a useful companion to reviews. Open FuelEconomy.gov EV comparison

How to use reviews

  • Read more than one source before forming an opinion about a model.
  • Separate objective details, such as range and cargo space, from personal preferences, such as styling or screen controls.
  • Check the model year, trim, battery size, and wheel size because those can change range and charging results.
  • Use WESTERN EV to summarize what to look for, then link to outside reviews for the detailed road-test work.
