Station Maps

Use established station-finder maps while WESTERN EV builds its own local reference material. Always confirm connector type, access, station status, and payment details before relying on one stop.

Station access basics

  • Access: Is the charger public, open now, and reachable without a gate, employee lot, paid garage, or restricted parking area?
  • Reliability: Check recent driver notes, broken-stall reports, and how many chargers are working.
  • Connector: Confirm CCS1, NACS, CHAdeMO, or Level 2 before routing there.
  • Adapter: If an adapter is needed, confirm that the station and vehicle both support that adapter.
  • Backup: For road trips, prefer major stations and know the next workable backup before leaving.

Outside charging-map sources

  • AFDC Station Locator: U.S. Department of Energy station locator for public alternative fueling and EV charging stations in the United States and Canada. Open AFDC locator
  • PlugShare: Widely used charging map with community check-ins, station details, filters, and driver notes. Open PlugShare
  • ChargePoint: Driver map for ChargePoint stations and other networked charging locations. Open ChargePoint map
  • Electrify America: Public fast-charging station locator with connector filters and station information. Open Electrify America locator
  • EVgo: Fast-charging station map and app links for EVgo locations. Open EVgo map
  • Tesla Find Us: Tesla map for Superchargers, destination chargers, service, and stores. Some Superchargers support non-Tesla EVs depending on vehicle, adapter, and site access. Open Tesla map
