Using Chargers

Public charging is much easier when the phone, apps, payment, and charger ID are ready before you reach the station.

Before the Trip

Prep
  • Install the network apps you expect to use.
  • Create accounts while you have good internet.
  • Add a payment card before leaving.
  • Sign in and confirm the app still opens.
  • Bring the phone, charging cable, and adapter.

At the Charger

Start
  • Check the station number or charger ID.
  • Match the plug to your car or adapter.
  • Follow the app or screen order carefully.
  • Confirm charging actually starts.
  • Watch the first minute for errors or slow power.

Phone and payment basics

  • Tesla Supercharger: Non-Tesla drivers usually need the Tesla app, vehicle details, and payment setup. Some sites allow card, mobile payment, or text-to-pay, but do not assume that is available.
  • Electrify America: The app shows live availability, connector types, and app payment. Guest charging can use debit or credit card swipe/tap at the charger.
  • ChargePoint: Common start options include app, ChargePoint card, phone tap, watch, QR code at some roaming stations, and contactless card or pay-by-phone at supported stations.
  • EVgo: The app shows plans and pricing. EVgo says drivers can use a credit card at the station, but fees may apply.
  • Reality check: Card readers, screens, cell service, and payment systems can fail. For road trips, app setup is still the safer plan.
