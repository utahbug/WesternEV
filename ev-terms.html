EV Terminology

A simple glossary for EV and charging words you are likely to see in manuals, apps, articles, and dashboard screens.

Quick glossary

  • EV: Electric vehicle.
  • BEV: Battery electric vehicle, powered only by a battery and electric motor.
  • PHEV: Plug-in hybrid electric vehicle, using both a rechargeable battery and gasoline engine.
  • ICE: Internal combustion engine, usually meaning a gas or diesel engine.
  • kW: Kilowatt, a measure of charging power or motor output.Example: a 150 kW charger can add energy faster than a 50 kW charger.
  • kWh: Kilowatt-hour, a measure of stored or used energy.Example: if a charger gives the car 30 kWh, think of that as the amount of energy added to the battery.
  • State of charge: The battery's current charge level, usually shown as a percentage.
  • Connector: The plug style used to connect the vehicle to a charger.
